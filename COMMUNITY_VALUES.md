# PeakVox — Community Values

The kind of community PeakVox wants to be, how we recognize the people who build it, and how we
think (carefully, and without promises) about contributor incentives and sustainability.

This complements the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) (the behavioral floor) and
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) (how decisions are made).

---

## Our values

- **Build in public.** Architecture, decisions, roadmap, and honest validation reports live in
  the open. We would rather be understood and challenged than admired and opaque.
- **Technical honesty.** We separate "the platform can orchestrate this" (*architecture-validated*)
  from "a real model runs this end to end" (*provider-validated*). We do not overstate support.
- **Respect for people's voices.** Voice is identity. We hold the line on consent and responsible
  use (see the [Voice Usage Policy](VOICE_USAGE_POLICY.md)) — in code, demos, and discussion.
- **Accessibility over gatekeeping.** Self-hosting is first-class. Capability should not require
  a credit card.
- **Disagree well.** Technical disagreement, argued specifically and respectfully, makes the
  project better. Personal attacks do not.
- **Credit generously.** The people who report bugs, write docs, test runtimes, and propose
  ideas are the project. We name them.

---

## How we recognize contributors today

Concrete, present-day recognition — not promises:

- **Attribution in history and release notes.** Merged contributions are credited; significant
  work is called out in the [CHANGELOG](CHANGELOG.md) and release notes.
- **Security credit.** Reporters of valid vulnerabilities are credited (with permission) in
  advisories — see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
- **Authorship of decisions.** Contributors who drive an architectural decision are named as
  deciders on the resulting ADR.
- **Visible influence.** Because the project is ADR-driven and built in public, a good proposal
  becomes part of the permanent, attributable record of how PeakVox is built.

## Future exploration: contributor recognition & sustainability

> **Read this carefully.** Everything in this section is an **exploration area**, not a
> guarantee. It describes directions the project *may* investigate as the community grows.
>
> **This section makes no financial promises. It is not a legal commitment. It does not
> describe, offer, or imply any token, equity, revenue share, payout, or financial
> instrument of any kind.** Nothing here should be relied upon for any decision.

As open-source projects mature, healthy ones think deliberately about how to recognize
sustained contribution and how to remain sustainable without compromising their values. PeakVox
expects to explore this *with* its community, in the open, when there is a community to explore
it with.

Areas that **may** be explored in the future include:

- **Contributor recognition** — richer, more visible ways to acknowledge sustained and
  high-impact contributions (e.g. maintainer pathways, contributor highlights).
- **Reputation systems** — lightweight, transparent ways to reflect trust and track record
  within the project and the Runtime Registry (for example, provenance and trust tiers for
  community-imported runtime variants already exist architecturally; see
  [ADR-0019](docs/.agents/DECISIONS/adr-0019-variant-trust-and-community-imports.md)).
- **Ecosystem incentives** — non-binding ideas for encouraging high-quality runtimes, voices,
  and integrations to flourish in the ecosystem.
- **Open-source sustainability models** — approaches that let the project remain maintained and
  independent over the long term, consistent with the open-core boundary.
- **Community participation programs** — structured ways for people to get involved beyond code:
  testing runtimes, validating providers, improving docs, triaging issues.

These are framed as **questions the project intends to ask**, not answers it has committed to.
If and when any of them become real, they will be proposed openly — through the
[governance process](GOVERNANCE.md) — with concrete terms, and never as a retroactive surprise.

## How to participate

You do not need to write code to be part of PeakVox:

- **Test and validate runtimes** — help move providers from architecture-validated to
  provider-validated.
- **Improve documentation** — clarity, examples, onboarding.
- **Triage and reproduce issues** — a clean reproduction is a real contribution.
- **Propose runtimes and model families** — see
  [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md#proposing-a-new-runtime-or-model-family).
- **Shape decisions** — weigh in on issues and ADRs.
