# Voice Usage Policy

PeakVox can synthesize and clone human voices. This capability is powerful and carries real potential for harm. This Voice Usage Policy sets out the responsibilities that apply to everyone who uses the software.

This policy is **incorporated into the [LICENSE](LICENSE) by reference** (Part C). Using PeakVox in violation of this policy is a violation of the license and terminates the rights granted to you.

> **Self-hosting note:** PeakVox is self-hosted software. The maintainers do not operate your deployment, cannot monitor how you use it, and are not responsible for content you generate. Responsibility rests entirely with the operator and the end user. See the [Liability Disclaimer](#liability-disclaimer).

---

## 1. Permitted Uses

You may use PeakVox for lawful, consensual, and good-faith purposes, including:

- Narration, audiobooks, podcasts, and other content using **your own voice** or voices you are **authorized** to use.
- Accessibility — e.g. assistive reading, communication aids, and giving a voice to those who cannot speak.
- Localization, dubbing, and language learning.
- Creative, artistic, and entertainment work where any cloned voice is used **with consent**.
- Prototyping, research, and education.
- Internal business uses such as IVR prompts, training material, and product voiceovers, using authorized voices.

When you clone or imitate a specific person's voice, you must have that person's **informed, documented consent** (or another clear lawful basis), and you must comply with applicable laws, including disclosure requirements for synthetic media.

---

## 2. Prohibited Uses

You may **not** use PeakVox to create, distribute, or facilitate:

- **Impersonation** of any real person or organization without their explicit consent.
- **Fraud, scams, or financial deception**, including voice-based social engineering.
- **Identity theft** or misappropriation of a person's voice, likeness, or identity.
- **Unauthorized voice cloning** — cloning any individual's voice without their informed consent.
- **Deceptive synthetic media** ("deepfakes") intended to mislead about authenticity, including fabricated statements attributed to real people.
- **Harassment, bullying, threats, intimidation, or defamation.**
- **Election interference or disinformation**, including fabricated statements by public figures presented as genuine.
- **Circumvention of voice authentication / biometric security** systems.
- Content that is **unlawful** where it is created, distributed, or consumed, or that violates the rights of others.
- Generation of content that exploits or endangers minors.

This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. Uses that are deceptive, non-consensual, or unlawful are prohibited even if not specifically listed.

---

## 3. User Responsibilities

As a user or operator of PeakVox, you are responsible for:

- **Obtaining consent.** Securing and retaining documented consent before cloning or imitating any real person's voice.
- **Lawful use.** Complying with all applicable laws and regulations in your jurisdiction, including those governing privacy, publicity/likeness rights, intellectual property, biometric data, consumer protection, and synthetic media disclosure.
- **Disclosure.** Clearly labeling AI-generated or AI-cloned audio where required by law or where a reasonable listener could be misled.
- **Securing your deployment.** Preventing unauthorized access to your instance and to the voice profiles and audio it stores (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)).
- **Downstream use.** If you operate PeakVox for others (where permitted by the license), enforcing this policy with your own users.

---

## 4. Voice Ownership Expectations

- A person's voice is an aspect of their identity. In many jurisdictions, individuals hold rights in their voice and likeness.
- You should treat any voice you did not record from a consenting speaker as **belonging to someone else**, and not use it without authorization.
- Reference audio you upload or record must be **yours to use** — either your own voice, a voice you have permission to use, or audio you are otherwise legally entitled to process.
- Outputs may be subject to third-party rights (e.g. the rights of the original speaker). Permission to use the software does not grant rights to any particular person's voice.

---

## 5. Consent Requirements

When cloning or imitating an identifiable person's voice, consent should be:

- **Informed** — the person understands their voice will be synthesized and how it will be used.
- **Specific** — covering the intended purposes and contexts of use.
- **Documented** — recorded in a form you can produce if asked.
- **Revocable** — honored if the person withdraws consent for future use.

Special care is required for vulnerable individuals. Cloning the voice of a minor requires verifiable consent from a parent or legal guardian and a lawful purpose.
